# Competitor Landing Notes

## Purpose

This file summarizes competitor positioning patterns that should inform the landing page. It is a design and messaging input, not a claim of exhaustive market analysis.

## Traderline

Observed positioning:

- Direct Polymarket/Betfair ladder trading.
- Desktop downloads.
- One-click execution.
- Practice mode.
- No subscription messaging.

Implication:

Do not claim that a prediction-market ladder does not exist. Differentiate through sharper scope, local/no-custody posture, risk gates, auditability, and serious live-readiness.

## Strata

Observed positioning:

- Desktop terminal.
- Kalshi first.
- Polymarket coming soon.
- Local-first credential/security narrative.
- Low latency and multi-market workspace.

Implication:

Security/local-first language is not optional. The landing must explain how credentials and execution are handled.

## Polyblock

Observed positioning:

- Polymarket terminal.
- Live order book and WebSocket.
- Instant execution.
- AI/analytics.
- Custodial wallet/server-side signing posture.

Implication:

A clear no-custody/manual-control wedge is valuable. Avoid competing on AI unless implemented and validated.

## PolyTraderPro

Observed positioning:

- Desktop Polymarket app.
- Faster trading.
- Multi-wallet.
- Anti-misclick controls.
- Cross-platform.

Implication:

Misclick/risk-control messaging matters. The landing should show risk limits, explicit live mode, and order audit trail.

## Native interfaces

Observed positioning:

- General-purpose market browsing and trading.
- Accessible to broad users.

Implication:

Our product should not compete as a beginner interface. It should position as a focused execution terminal for order-book traders.
